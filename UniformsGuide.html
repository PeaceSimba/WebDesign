Uniform Requirements:-

Simplicity is one of the distinguishing characteristics of a truly fine dancer. While we're working on the strength and technique needed to accomplish this goal, we can start by being simple in dress, which is why we have a uniform. As a conservatory level school of dance our dress code is an important part of our curriculum. Showing respect for our art in conduct as well as a dress code helps foster the required discipline it takes to learn a classical art. To assist you in preparing your dancer properly for classes following are specifics on our dress code



Ballet(girls);

  • Black leotard of any style. Simple designs are always the best.
  • Bloch Salmon Tight either footed or convertible. Seams required for Student and Advanced Division for performances only.
  • Pink ballet slippers by Bloch. The shoes selected must match the color of the Bloch Salmon tights. Elastics MUST be sewn on ballet slippers. The elastics are needed for safety - in an attempt to keep the shoes from falling off, the dancer will unconsciously grip the ligaments of the foot, which can lead to strain and possible injury.




  • Hair in a classical ballet bun. If you need bun making lessons just ask, we are happy to help. A proper bun secured tightly to the head is important especially when turning in class. Hair that is falling out is disruptive to the learning process and is also a safety hazard as pins fall to the floor.
  • NO DANGLING EARRINGS. This is a safety requirement. No rings, bracelets, or necklaces. Please leave your jewelry at home.

  • Pointe Shoes for beginner pointe dancers should NOT be purchased until they are given guidelines by the teacher. Elastics and ribbons must be properly sewn to the pointe shoes once they are approved by the teacher. A tutorial on how to properly sew pointe shoes will be given by NYAB staff as needed. NO STREET CLOTHES allowed in class. I understand the need for additional clothing in the cold weather. If your dancer does not own a ballet sweater, NYAB has loaner sweaters in the girls dressing room. Additionally, in the cold weather please keep all warm-up clothing to the colors of black and pink. Simple black and pink exemplifies the conservatory level work in the studio at LBDC

*Special note for our Saturday Student Division and Advanced Division dancers. Saturday is an extra special day at LBDC so if you like please wear a pastel colored leotard and you may also wear a chiffon skirt.


Uniform Ballet Requirements all Ballet Divisions (Boys):

  • Plain white T-Shirt tucked into tights.
  • Young boys may wear bike shorts and a white T-Shirt tucked in. However, as they mature, dance belts and black tights are required.
  • Black ballet shoes with elastic sewn on and white socks.
  • Hair neatly combed back out of face and secured.

Jazz:

You may wear a colored leotard with black jazz pants. If you are taking ballet directly before or after your jazz class please wear your ballet uniform to jazz class which you can cover up with a pair of black jazz pants if you so desire. Black lace up jazz shoes. Hair pulled back off face. No T-shirts or other street clothes allowed

Modern:

Black footless tights or black mid-thigh length athletic shorts worn over a black leotard. Bare feet. Hair pulled back off face. No street clothes allowed.

Hip-Hop:

Express yourself! Flat bottom clean sneakers that you do NOT wear outside. No black soled sneakers as they leave marks on our marley floor.

Contemporary:

​​Express yourself!! Leotard of any color with tights or athletic shorts. Black or white T-shirt/tank, with black or white athletic shorts. Bare Feet or socks.
